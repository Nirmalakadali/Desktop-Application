Tuticorin

Tuticorin, sometimes referred to as Thoothukudi, is a port city in Tamil Nadu's Thoothukudi district. On the Gulf of Mannar on the Bay of Bengal, the city is located 40 kilometres east of Tirunelveli. Due to the pearl fishing in the area, Tuticorin is referred to as the "pearl city."

History:

Tamil Nadu was one of the first of British settlements in India. The State is the successor to the old Madras Presidency, which covered the bulk of the southern peninsula in 1901. The composite Madras State was later reorganised, and the present Tamil Nadu was formed.

Speciality

A major part of Tamil performing arts is its classical form of dance, the Bharatanatyam, whereas the popular forms are known as Koothu. Classical Tamil music is dominated by the Carnatic genre, while gaana and dappan koothu are also popular genres. Tamil is an official language in Sri Lanka and Singapore

Location

Tamil Nadu is located in the southernmost part of the Indian subcontinent. It is bounded by the states of Andhra Pradesh to the north, Karnataka to the northwest, and Kerala to the west and the Indian Ocean to the east and south.
