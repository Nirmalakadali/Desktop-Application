History:

After the Indian independence from British Empire in 1947, the land became Lushai Hills district under the Government of Assam. In 1972 the district was declared a union territory and was given a more culturally inclusive name Mizoram. Ultimately Mizoram became a full-fledged federal state of India in 1986.

Speciality

Mizoram is also known for its famous cultural festivals, like the Chapchar Kut Festival, Mim Kut Festival, Pawl Kut festival, and many more. These festivals also attract a lot of tourists to visit this North-Eastern State of India. Mizo culture is known for its music and dance.

Location

Mizoram, state of India. It is located in the northeastern part of the country and is bounded by Myanmar (Burma) to the east and south and Bangladesh to the west and by the states of Tripura to the northwest, Assam to the north, and Manipur to the northeast.
