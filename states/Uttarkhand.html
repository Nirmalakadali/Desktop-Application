History:

In the Puranas, Uttarakhand was the ancient term for the central Indian Himalayas. Its peaks and valleys were known as Svarga loka: a temporary abode of the righteous, and the source of the Ganges. At that time, present-day Uttarakhand was also reportedly inhabited by rishis and sadhus.

Speciality

Uttarakhand, a state in northern India crossed by the Himalayas, is known for its Hindu pilgrimage sites. Rishikesh, a major centre for yoga study, was made famous by the Beatles' 1968 visit. The city hosts the evening Ganga Aarti, a spiritual gathering on the sacred Ganges River.

Location

The state is located in the northern part of India, bordered by the Indian state of Himachal Pradesh in north-west, by the Autonomous region of Tibet (China) in north-east, by Nepal in south-east, and by Uttar Pradesh (India) in south-west. Uttarakhand has a short border with Haryana in west.
